25-1-2017
The last few weeks we started learning javascript. The current goal is to make our memory game work. Every week we learn a little bit more so the game develops together with our skills in javascript. Thus far we learned about

  • - object oriented programming, what are objects, how to access them
  • - using this, var,
  • - if/else , for loops
  • - working with arrays
  • - private and public objects

The game works in the console of the browser for now. A player can choose two cards, the game checks if the cards are still on the table, if the cards are turned upside down ans if the cards are the samen. If two cards are the same, the player can play again and the cards are removed from the table. If the cards are different, the game switches to the other player. For now it is not possible to assign a score to the players. The homework for our next meeting is to make it possible to visualise the gameplay in the browser. I have been working on this for the past evenings. It still isn't clear enough for me how to do it, so I hope I will learn more tomorrow.
I still enjoy learning everything very much. At one hand I am proud to see how much I've already learned in the past few weeks, at the other hand I get frustrated about how much effort it costs me to grasp what I am doing and how to continue in the programme. The frustration is a positieve felling, though. It shows me I am developing myself and I am really learning. I underestimated how difficult it is to combine this learning with running a family and having a job that takes my attention during daytime. So all the learning I do, I have to do in the evening, after the kids are asleep and when my head is already a bit tired. I think I can be proud of the progress I make nonetheless.
